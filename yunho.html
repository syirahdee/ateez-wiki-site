Jeong Yun-ho (Korean: 정윤호; born March 23, 1999), mononymously known as Yunho, is a South Korean singer, dancer and actor. He debuted as one of the main dancers and vocalists for the South Korean boy band Ateez in 2018.

EARLY LIFE

Jeong Yun-ho was born March 23, 1999, in Gwangju, South Korea. He developed an interest in singing and performing at a young age and studied at local music and dance academies in Gwangju. He later moved to Seoul to attend the School of Performing Arts Seoul (SOPA), where he studied in the applied music department, before enrolling at the Global Cyber University.

CAREER

2017-2019: PRE-DEBUT AND DEBUT WITH ATEEZ

Prior to his official debut, Yunho trained as part of KQ Fellaz, a pre-debut team formed by KQ Entertainment. He debuted with Ateez on October 24, 2018, when the group released their first EP, Treasure EP.1: All to Zero.

2020-PRESENT: ACTING DEBUT, IDOL RADIO AND OSTs

On October 16, 2020, it was officially announced that Yunho would make his formal acting debut in the KBS2 drama Imitation, a television adaptation of the popular webtoon. He took on the prominent role of the second male lead, Lee Yu-jin, the sweet and fiercely determined center of the fictional K-pop boy group Sparkling. Throughout the series, which aired from May 7 to July 23, 2021, Yunho earned praise for his compelling portrayal of a devoted friend navigating the intense pressures of the idol industry while harboring unrequited feelings for the female lead.

In addition to showcasing his acting abilities, Yunho actively lent his vocal talents to the drama's original soundtrack (OST). In particular, he featured in the high-energy track "Diamond," released under the fictional group Sparkling, and also collaborated with lead actress Jung Ji-so on the heartfelt duet "Like When We First Met." Performed entirely in character as Yujin and Maha, the romantic song beautifully captured their complex on-screen chemistry and added emotional depth to the series.

On September 8, 2022, MBC officially announced that Yunho, alongside fellow Ateez bandmate Hongjoong, would be taking over as the new official DJs for the highly anticipated third season of the popular radio program, Idol Radio, fondly dubbed by fans and listeners as "Yundj." The duo brought their signature energy, deep industry insight, and natural chemistry to the late-night broadcast, creating a welcoming space for various K-pop artists to showcase their talents and connect with fans. Their successful tenure came to a bittersweet close a year later, on December 22, 2023, when their final episode as regular hosts was aired. The emotional broadcast marked their official departure from the show, concluding an impressive and beloved chapter as the voices guiding the late-night K-pop community.

On May 25, 2024, Yunho threw the first pitch for the Kia Tigers in a match against the Doosan Bears in Gwangju. On August 7, Yunho appeared in the music video for the song "Kang Kang Sulri" by Lee Doo-Soo. On October 2, Yunho released the song "I'm Alright" with a lyric video.

On June 7, 2025, Yunho was once again invited by the Kia Tigers to throw the first pitch in a game against the Lotte Giants.

On July 5, Yunho released the soundtrack "On My Way to You" for the KBS drama For Eagle Brothers. On July 25, it was revealed that Yunho was cast in 건전지무선전화, a short drama released to commemorate the 20th anniversary of MBC Challenge! It was released on the Kante Japan app on August 8, 2025.

On July 20, 2025, CJ ENM unveiled the teaser trailer for the musical short film Backstage, confirming Ateez's Yunho in his big-screen acting debut. Directed by Kang Seung-won, the 23-minute project introduced an innovative "music short movie" format. The entire film was shot using the iPhone 16 Pro, delivering a raw, poetic, visually crisp cinematic experience that captured the raw intensity of youth.

Backstage centers on Baekho (played by ATEEZ's Yunho), a talented vocalist unexpectedly asked to perform with the band that betrayed him just two years prior. Stepping backstage just moments before showtime, he is forced to confront unresolved emotional wounds, tense conflicts, and physical altercations with his former bandmates before they can unite through their shared music. Backed by a high-caliber production crew and a soundtrack from the acclaimed CJ ENM OST team (Lonely Gourmet, Crash Landing on You), the film made its exclusive premiere in South Korean CGV theaters on August 18, 2025, leaving a lasting impression on moviegoers and fans alike.

FASHION

Expanding Domestic Influence

Yunho's fashion footprint began gaining major traction on September 7, 2024, when he attended the Kwanghyuk Spring/Summer 2025 Collection show during Seoul Fashion Week, showcasing his support for contemporary South Korean design. Building on this momentum, his relationship with international luxury labels deepened the following year. On July 2, 2025, he was invited to the high-profile opening of the Italian luxury house Ferragamo's new boutique at The Hyundai Seoul, turning heads at the official photocall in a sophisticated ensemble that perfectly balanced classic Italian tailoring with modern edge.

Global Runway Debut at Milan Fashion Week

His fashion trajectory reached a major international milestone on February 24, 2026, when Yunho made his highly anticipated Milan Fashion Week debut at the Diesel Fall/Winter 2026 show. Making waves as the only South Korean artist present, he stole the spotlight in a bold, tailored leather outfit that seamlessly aligned with Diesel's gritty, avant-garde aesthetic. Commanding the venue with effortless confidence amidst a flurry of camera flashes, Yunho drew immense global praise, firmly solidifying his status as a versatile fashion icon on both domestic and international stages.
