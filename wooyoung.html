Jung Wooyoung (Korean: 정우영; born November 26, 1999) known mononymously as Wooyoung, is a South Korean vocalist and dancer. He is a member of the boy group Ateez, formed by KQ Entertainment.

EARLY LIFE

He attended Hanlim Multi Arts High School. In 2014 he started as a trainee at BigHit Music (formerly known as BigHit Entertainment). During this time, he crossed paths with Yeosang. Wooyoung decided to follow Yeosang and join him at KQ Entertainment in 2017. In October 2017, a few of KQ Entertainment's trainees took part in a casting for the show MIXNINE (a survival reality show). Unfortunately, Wooyoung couldn't make it.

CAREER

2017: MIXNINE

In October 2017, trainees from KQ Entertainment auditioned for the survival reality show MIXNINE. Wooyoung is one of the four boys who passed the audition. He ranked 57 in the MIXNINE Just Dance showcase but was eliminated in the seventh episode. His final ranking is number 132.

2018: KQ FELLAZ, DEBUT IN ATEEZ

Before officially taking the stage, Wooyoung spent his trainee days as part of KQ Fellaz, the pre-debut team under KQ Entertainment. During this time, he built essential chemistry with his future bandmates and showcased his raw talents through the viral KQ Fellaz Performance Video 1, which immediately drew international eyes to the group's intense choreography and synchronized skills.

On October 24, 2018, that preparation culminated in his official debut as a member of ATEEZ with the dual title tracks "Treasure" and "Pirate King" from their first mini-album. Within the group, Wooyoung established himself as a vital core of the performance line and a steady vocalist. Known for his incredibly fluid, expressive dancing and crisp execution, he frequently anchors ATEEZ's high-energy choreographies, while his clear, smooth vocal tone adds a distinct texture to the group's diverse discography.

FILMOGRAPHY

While Wooyoung primarily focuses on group activities, his definitive solo milestone came in June 2021 when he was selected as Studio Choom's "Artist of the Month," a prestigious spotlight reserved for K-pop's elite dancers. Delivering a mesmerizing, fluid dance cover of Christopher's "Bad,"

he effortlessly blended raw charisma with sharp precision, creating a viral sensation that rapidly amassed millions of views on YouTube. The performance broke records as one of the platform's fastest-growing features, firmly solidifying Wooyoung's reputation as one of the premier main dancers of his generation.

FASHION

Wooyoung's rapid ascent in the global fashion industry highlights his natural transition from a powerful stage performer to a front-row fixture at major European fashion capitals. By participating in four consecutive major shows across Paris and Milan, he has proved his versatility, developing an ongoing creative relationship with the French house Courrèges and expanding into major legacy houses like Prada.

The Courrèges Evolution (Paris Fashion Week)

His journey as a fashion icon began in September 2024 at the Courrèges Spring/Summer 2025 presentation. Making his front-row debut, Wooyoung leaned into a structured, refined denim-on-denim look that perfectly balanced high fashion with a wearable, retro-modern edge. The appearance drew massive crowds outside the venue, proving his instant drawing power.

Courrèges quickly brought him back for the Fall/Winter 2025 show in March 2025, where Wooyoung delivered an entirely different, incredibly daring aesthetic. Stripping away conventional layers, he wore an open, all-leather jacket entirely shirtless underneath.

This bold choice went viral across social media, showcasing his confidence and perfectly embodying the brand's sleek, rebellious energy. By the time he returned for the Spring/Summer 2026 showcase in September 2025, he did so as a key featured brand ambassador, commanding the venue in a polished, monochrome all-black utility look that solidified his long-term partnership with the brand.

Expanding to Milan (Prada Debut)

Building on his sustained momentum in Paris, Wooyoung took his talents to Italy in February 2026 for Milan Fashion Week. Attending the highly exclusive Prada Fall/Winter 2026/2027 womenswear presentation, his arrival signaled an expansion into the absolute upper echelon of luxury fashion. Walking into the venue, he easily adapted to Prada's signature sophisticated, high-concept minimalism. His presence at the star-studded event generated massive media impressions, cementing his status as a truly versatile global style icon who effortlessly commands attention across both men's and women's fashion capitals.
